# CS 230 Portfolio Reflection – The Gaming Room

## Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements.

The client for this project was The Gaming Room. They wanted to take their game, *Draw It or Lose It*, which only worked on Android, and make it into a web-based game that people could play on different devices and operating systems. The game also needed to support multiple teams and players while making sure every game, team, and player had a unique name.

## What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?

I think I did a good job explaining why I chose certain design options and comparing different operating systems. I also made sure to include the client's requirements throughout the document so my recommendations matched what they were looking for.

## What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?

The design document helped me think through the project before writing any code. It made me focus on the overall structure first instead of jumping straight into programming. Having a plan made it easier to understand how everything would work together.

## If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

If I could go back, I would spend more time on the system design section. I would add more diagrams and explain some of my decisions in more detail so someone else reading the document could follow my thought process more easily.

## How did you interpret the user's needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user's needs when designing?

I focused on what the client wanted the software to do and made sure the design supported those goals. Since they wanted the game to work across different platforms and support multiple users, I recommended a web-based design that would be easier to maintain and expand. It's important to think about the user's needs because the software has to solve their problem, not just work correctly.

## How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?

I started by reading through the requirements and breaking the project into smaller pieces. Then I looked at different design options and chose the ones that made the most sense for the client's needs. In the future, I would follow the same process by planning everything out first, comparing different solutions, and creating a design before I start coding. I think taking the time to plan makes development much smoother.
